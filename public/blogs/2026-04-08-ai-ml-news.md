## Amazon S3 Files: Object store becomes file system.

AWS has launched S3 Files, a new feature that transforms Amazon S3, the largest object store, into a functional file system. This allows users to treat S3 storage as if it were a traditional file system.

## AI platform streamlines healthcare access and data.

Rubrum Solutions has launched a new AI platform designed to streamline healthcare processes like prior authorization and benefits verification by organizing health data in real-time. This innovation aims to accelerate patient access to innovative treatments by making clinical and real-world evidence more readily available.

## NVIDIA DLSS 5: AI-enhanced real-time graphics synthesis

NVIDIA's upcoming DLSS 5 technology, unveiled at GTC 2026, will go beyond current AI enhancements to achieve fully AI-synthesized real-time graphics. It promises a significant leap in visual fidelity with photorealistic lighting and materials through advanced neural networks.

## AI Capability Exceeds Control, Risking Reliability

The real risk in AI stems not from its capabilities, but from a dangerous lack of control, with issues like weak monitoring and overtrusting powerful models posing threats as AI integrates into the real world. Future AI leaders will prioritize building reliable, auditable, and trustworthy systems over simply developing more intelligent models.

## AI-driven brute force: Rate limiting is dead.

AI-powered brute-force cyberattacks are rapidly increasing, with a nearly 89% year-over-year rise and an estimated 11,000 attacks per second by early 2026. This escalating threat renders traditional rate-limiting security measures obsolete due to the sophistication and volume of AI-driven attacks.
