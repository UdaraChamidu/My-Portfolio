## Cisco SD-WAN bugs exploited as 0-day.

A critical "make-me-root" vulnerability in Cisco SD-WAN is currently under active attack, and this marks the second zero-day exploit targeting a Cisco Catalyst SD-WAN Manager flaw this month. This indicates a significant security risk for users of these Cisco products.

## AI safety researchers criticize Fable 5 fix code incident.

A researcher claims that federal agents overreacted to OpenAI's Fable 5 AI after a simple coding request, not a malicious jailbreak attempt. This reaction, according to the researcher, demonstrates a misunderstanding of the AI's capabilities and the security testing conducted.

## AI data hunger outstrips storage innovation.

AI is driving significant changes in how data is handled, but traditional storage systems are struggling to keep pace with AI's insatiable demand. This gap means that the powerful GPUs essential for AI development are often left waiting for data, hindering progress.

## DARPA seeks swappable satellites for space defense.

DARPA is exploring swappable satellites to ensure critical orbital systems can be quickly replaced after a potential "star wars" attack. They are assessing industry's capacity for rapid launches of replacement satellites.
