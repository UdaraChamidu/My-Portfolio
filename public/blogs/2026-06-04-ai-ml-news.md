## Beam manipulation defeats anti-jamming signals.

Rice University researchers have developed a method to manipulate radio beams, potentially allowing signals to circumvent anti-jamming technology by making their source difficult to pinpoint. This "beam bending" technique could overcome challenges posed by signal jammers.

## Open data architecture fuels DoorDash's logistics and AI.

DoorDash is leveraging an open data architecture to power its real-time logistics and ambitious agentic AI initiatives. This approach is crucial for large-scale operations, enabling both machine learning and analytics on a unified, scalable infrastructure.

## AI blamed for graduate job woes, but unemployment rose earlier.

New research from the New York Federal Reserve suggests that rising graduate unemployment predates the widespread adoption of AI, implying AI is being unfairly blamed for the trend. An alternative, unstated job perk may be a more significant factor in graduate hiring prospects.

## Microsoft Adds Linux Commands to Windows

Microsoft is bringing a significant portion of Linux's core command-line utilities, Coreutils, to Windows. This integration allows users to run over 75 common Unix commands within Windows and PowerShell environments.

---

## Ring sued for collecting facial data without consent

Ring is facing a class-action lawsuit for allegedly collecting visitor facial data without consent. This latest legal action is part of a growing trend of scrutiny over the privacy implications of AI technologies like Ring's "Familiar Faces" feature.
