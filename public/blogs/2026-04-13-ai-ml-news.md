## AI layoffs fuel cybersecurity startup funding surge.

Recent AI-driven tech layoffs, totaling 15,000 jobs on April 12, 2026, have significantly boosted funding for cybersecurity startups. This surge, with a 35% increase in investment, is directly attributed to the growing need for solutions addressing AI-related risks.

## Data drift: Undermining AI security models

Data drift occurs when the data used to train an ML model changes, reducing its accuracy and creating security vulnerabilities. Cybersecurity teams can detect drift through declining model performance, shifts in data distributions, changes in prediction behavior, increased model uncertainty, and altered feature relationships, necessitating continuous monitoring and model retraining.

## AI funding crunch for startups, YC targets Indian talent.

Large AI companies are securing most funding, leaving little for smaller startups. Y Combinator is looking to India's talent pool to foster new global-focused startups in sectors like fintech, consumer, and B2B.

## AI agent automates ML research trial-and-error

Andrej Karpathy's AutoResearch Agent automates the ML experimentation loop, allowing an AI to modify code, run tests, and log results overnight. After 40+ experiments, the agent demonstrated creative architectural tweaks, suggesting the future of ML lies in effective instruction for AI agents.
