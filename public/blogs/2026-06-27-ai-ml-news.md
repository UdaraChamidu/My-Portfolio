## Google seeks AI regulation, on its own terms.

Google is advocating for AI regulation that aligns with its current development and business practices. The company believes it's possible to establish rules that permit them to continue their AI endeavors without significant disruption.

---

## AI provider lock-in concerns senior executives.

A recent IBM study reveals that over 70% of senior executives are concerned about AI system lock-in, finding it challenging to switch from their current AI providers. This widespread difficulty in transitioning highlights a significant barrier to flexibility and competition in the AI landscape.

## Yeedi S20 Ultra: Near-flawless premium robot vacuum performance

The Yeedi S20 Infinity Ultra is a high-performing robot vacuum that excels at both vacuuming and mopping, offering a user-friendly experience and innovative features like its 'Jet Spray' function. It delivers an "almost faultless" cleaning performance, making it a premium option.

## AI drug discovery clinical proof, China biotech challenge

AI drug discovery has achieved clinical proof with a successful Phase IIa trial, signaling a move from speculation to evidence. Meanwhile, China's biotech innovation in originating drug molecules presents a significant strategic challenge, as it now competes in discovery rather than just manufacturing.

## Amazon hikes AI cloud prices again.

Amazon Web Services (AWS) is increasing its AI cloud prices by 20%, following a previous 15% hike in January. This move makes AI services significantly more expensive on the platform.
