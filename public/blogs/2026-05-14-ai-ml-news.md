## **Critical MCP/Apache/Alibaba Flaws, One Vendor Unpatched**

Security researchers discovered critical vulnerabilities in three major MCPs, but one vendor has refused to issue a fix. Additionally, Apache and Alibaba databases are also affected, with only Apache providing a patch.

## AI use diminishes executive value of human workers.

Despite AI's inconsistent ROI, many executives now value human workers less after implementing the technology. This suggests a growing perception that AI can replace human contributions, even while failing to consistently prove its financial benefits.

## Radar virtual touch, AI, and diverse North Texas patents.

Samsung has patented a radar-based virtual touch interaction system for any display device. The North Texas region also saw other notable patents granted, including those from AT&T for machine learning feature discovery and Bank of America for synthetic data generation.

## Meta workers revolt over AI mouse tracking, fearing job cuts.

Meta employees are protesting new mouse-tracking technology, expressing concerns about privacy and the potential for this AI-training tool to lead to job losses. Flyers highlight worker anxieties about being at an "Employee Data Extraction Factory."
